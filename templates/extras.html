Animated Puppet

This animated puppet uses live face tracking in real time to produce a lively, vibrant character. When not detecting motion from the camera, the puppet plays ambient animations to simulate breathing and idle motion.

This puppet project began as a simple sketch and the drive to learn about interactive motion design. The primary software used was Adobe Photoshop, Adobe Illustrator, and Live2D.

The puppet is composed of many pieces, each illustrated independently and mirrored as necessary.

The mesh of the puppet, pictured above, has variations of complexity based on whether the piece needs to deform with a head turn.

Lens Photography

The final piece of my bonus portfolio - my love letter to the theatres. I was one of the last of the film projectionists, and I had the privilege of taking many of the projector parts home with me when the last film projectors were disassembled. I chose a Christie 50mm scope lens as my subject. Years of use had filled this lens with dust and blemishes, but in my eyes, the beauty of it is unmatched. I still keep all the old projector pieces - my heart beats in the magic of film.

Thank you for reviewing my work!
